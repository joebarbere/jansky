# Landmark Papers, Year by Year

A year-by-year timeline of radio astronomy through its most influential papers — roughly the
**top three per year** since Karl Jansky's founding work. It complements the thematic
[Bibliography](references.md) (which groups the field's seminal papers by topic); this page is
chronological, so you can trace how the field unfolded.

!!! note "How this list was built"
    "Top three" is a curated judgement based on historical impact and citations, not a
    formula — reasonable people will quibble, and the modern years (thousands of radio papers
    annually) are necessarily a sample of the landmarks. Every entry was **link-verified**
    against [NASA ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/), arXiv, or a publisher DOI at compile
    time (mid-2026); papers that could not be verified were left out rather than guessed. The
    earliest decades were genuinely sparse — some years list fewer than three (or none),
    because that is the honest historical record, not an omission.

Links go to the **ADS** abstract or the **DOI**. First authors are shown as "et al."; follow
the link for the full author list.

## 1930s — the founding

Radio astronomy is born, then lies almost dormant: between Jansky's discovery and Grote Reber,
almost no one pursued it. *1934, 1936, and 1938–39 have no major radio-astronomy papers.*

| Year | Authors | Title | Journal | Why it matters | Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1932 | Jansky, K. G. | Directional Studies of Atmospherics at High Frequencies | Proc. IRE 20, 1920 | The system that detected a steady "hiss" of unknown origin — the seed of the field | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1109/JRPROC.1932.227477) |
| 1933 | Jansky, K. G. | Electrical Disturbances Apparently of Extraterrestrial Origin | Proc. IRE 21, 1387 | Established the static came from beyond the solar system — the founding paper | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1109/JRPROC.1933.227458) |
| 1933 | Jansky, K. G. | Radio Waves from Outside the Solar System | Nature 132, 66 | Brought the discovery of cosmic radio emission to the wider scientific world | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/132066a0) |
| 1935 | Jansky, K. G. | A Note on the Source of Interstellar Interference | Proc. IRE 23, 1158 | Localised the emission to the Milky Way / Galactic plane | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1109/JRPROC.1935.227275) |
| 1937 | Whipple, F. L. & Greenstein, J. L. | On the Origin of Interstellar Radio Disturbances | PNAS 23, 177 | First theory of Jansky's emission (thermal; fell short by ~10⁴, motivating non-thermal models) | [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.23.3.177) |
