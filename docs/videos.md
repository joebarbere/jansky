# Watch on YouTube

A curated, hand-verified library of channels and videos that pair with the course. Every video
below was confirmed live via YouTube's oEmbed endpoint, every channel URL was confirmed to
resolve, and each topic group is **cross-referenced** to the chapter (and other pages) it
supports. Titles are quoted as YouTube returns them.

!!! tip "Two ways in"
    Prefer a short, hand-picked set embedded as thumbnails? See the
    **[Visual Tour](visual-tour.md#watch-learn)**. This page is the fuller library, organised by
    topic and channel.

### History & foundations

> **Pairs with** [Chapter 1 · What is Radio Astronomy?](notebooks/01_what_is_radio_astronomy.ipynb) and the [References](references.md).

- **[NRAO Jansky Lecture 1975: Dr. Grote Reber, Beginning of Radio Astronomy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xjG-97DgbO0)** — NRAO — The founder of dish radio astronomy tells the origin story in his own words.
- **[John Kraus: "Grote Reber, Founder of Radio Astronomy"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oeqR3YGDDq4)** — Canadian Radio Astronomy History — A pioneering radio astronomer recounts Reber's backyard work.
- **[What is Radio Astronomy?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NvNANHkLjhk)** — Dunlap Institute — A concise primer on what radio astronomy is and why we do it.
- **[Beyond the Visible](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_MCEVFtckKE)** — NSF/NRAO — Jodie Foster narrates the story of the Very Large Array and the radio sky.
- **[Jodrell Bank: the telescope that watched the Space Race](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=waxMOcACpWA)** — Space Mog — The history of the iconic Lovell Telescope and early radio astronomy.
